> Query 01: What is the most efficient processing unit in the known universe?

The Biological Neuron.

Inside your brain right now, 86 billion neurons are communicating. A biological neuron receives signals through its "dendrites".

If the combined signal is strong enough, it breaches a threshold and "fires", sending an electrical impulse down its axon to the next neuron.

> Query 02: How do you teach a rock (silicon) to think?

The Artificial Perceptron.

In the 1950s, scientists mapped biology into mathematics. An Artificial Neuron takes numerical inputs, multiplies them by a "weight" (importance), and sums them up.

If the math passes a specific threshold, it outputs a 1. Otherwise, a 0.

> Query 03: One neuron is useless. What happens when billions of blind mathematical nodes whisper to each other?

Emergence. (The Neural Network)

By stacking thousands of neurons in layers, the output of one becomes the input of the next. Through massive trial and error (training), the network automatically adjusts its "weights".

Suddenly, basic math equations can recognize a face, write a poem, or debug code. It learns.
